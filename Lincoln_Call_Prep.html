What to Know Going In

▼
NCAA Division II: Higher level than NAIA. D2 programs can offer up to 9 athletic scholarships per baseball team. Real money could be on the table.
Oldest HBCU in America: Founded 1854. Alumni include Langston Hughes and Thurgood Marshall. Deep history and strong campus culture.
CACC Conference: Baseball recently moved to the Central Atlantic Collegiate Conference. Opponents in PA, NJ, and the mid-Atlantic.
Coach Connor is a pitching specialist: He dramatically improved Lincoln's pitching numbers as an assistant. A pitching-focused coach values a catcher who can manage a staff — that's Aiden's strength.
On-campus housing: 95% of students live on campus. True residential HBCU experience. Room & board ~$15,000/year.
Engineering Science: Lincoln offers an Engineering Science B.S. Need to ask what that covers and where graduates go — it may differ from traditional Civil/Electrical/Mechanical engineering programs.
Small school: ~1,400 undergrads. Smaller classes, more attention, but check on resources and baseball facilities.
Rural location: ~45 miles SW of Philadelphia. Beautiful but isolated — different vibe from New Orleans or Boston.

Lincoln-Specific Questions to Ask

▼
About the Film & Fit
What stood out to you when you watched my film, and where do you see me fitting in with your program?
How many catchers are on the roster right now, and how many are returning?
What does the path to playing time look like for an incoming freshman catcher?
About the Program
How has the move to the CACC conference changed things for the program?
What's the vision for Lincoln baseball in the next few years?
What does a typical week look like during the season — practice, games, travel?
What are the facilities like — field, batting cages, weight room?
I know your background is in pitching development — how important is the catcher relationship to your pitching philosophy?This is a great question for Coach Connor specifically. It shows Aiden understands the game.
About Engineering at Lincoln
I'm interested in Engineering — can you tell me about the Engineering Science program and how athletes balance it with the season?
Where do Engineering Science graduates typically go — grad school, industry, or further study?
Are there other athletes in the engineering program?
What academic support is available — tutoring, study hall, advisors for athletes?
About Scholarships & Money
As a D2 program, what kind of athletic scholarship is available for baseball?
Can athletic aid stack with academic scholarships or need-based aid?
What does the realistic out-of-pocket cost look like for an out-of-state student-athlete after all aid?
Is on-campus housing required for freshmen, and what does room and board include?
About Next Steps
Can we schedule a campus visit?
Do you run prospect camps or fall workouts for incoming players?
What's your recruiting timeline — when would you need a decision?
