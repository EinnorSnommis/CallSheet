Questions to Ask the Coach

▼
These show you've done your homework and care about fit — not just getting an offer.
How many catchers are currently on your roster, and how many return next year?
What does the path to playing time look like for an incoming freshman?
Are you recruiting other catchers in this class?
What stood out to you when you watched my film?
What did you like about what you saw, and where do you see me fitting in with your program?
What's a typical week like for players during the season? Offseason?
How would you describe the culture you're building in this program?
How does your program support players in demanding majors like Engineering?
What athletic and academic scholarship or financial aid is available?
Can I schedule a campus visit or attend a prospect workout?
What's your recruiting timeline — when do you need a commitment decision by?

Questions Coaches Will Ask Me

▼
Be honest and direct. Coaches respect authenticity over rehearsed answers.
What's your pop time? Fastball velocity?Have numbers ready, or say "I'll get verified numbers to you."
Are you committed anywhere else, or talking to other schools?Be honest — mention others. It shows you're in demand.
What position do you see yourself playing in college?
Tell me about your game — what kind of hitter are you? How do you manage a pitching staff?
Why are you interested in our program specifically?Do your homework on each school before the call.
What do you want to study?Engineering — know what each school offers.
What's your GPA? SAT/ACT scores?GPA is 3.732 — strong. Have test scores ready if taken.
Can your high school coach speak on your behalf?"Yes — Coach Gamble is available." Share his contact info.
Do you have additional film or a full game we can watch?
Can you come visit campus?
